\section{Linear Forms/Q.2.21}
The perpendicular from the origin to a line
meets it at a point $\myvec{-2 \\ 9}$, find the equation of
the line.
\section{Solution}
Let the equation of line be
\begin{align}
    \myvec{-m & 1}\vec{x}=c
\end{align}
So the perpendicular from the origin meets the line at $\vec{P}= \myvec{-2 \\ 9}$.Since,
\begin{align}
    \vec{P}-\vec{O}=\vec{P}
\end{align}
is the normal vector where $\vec{O}$ is the origin then 
\begin{align}
    \vec{m}=\myvec{1 \\ m}
\end{align}
is the direction vector, Hence
\begin{align}
    \vec{m}^\top \vec{P}=0\\
    \implies \myvec{1 & m}\myvec{-2 \\ 9}=0\\
    \implies -2+9m=0\\
    \implies m = \frac{2}{9}
\end{align}
Now the line
\begin{align}
    \myvec{-m & 1}\vec{x}=c
\end{align}
meets it at the point $\vec{P}=\myvec{-2 \\9}$ ,Using m.we get,
\begin{align}
    \myvec{\frac{-2}{9} & 1}\vec{P}=c \\
    \implies \myvec{\frac{-2}{9} & 1}\myvec{-2 \\9}=c\\
    \implies \frac{4}{9}+9=c\\
    \implies c=\frac{85}{9}
\end{align}
Hence, the values of m and c are obtains as 
$$m=\frac{2}{9}, c =\frac{85}{9}$$
respectives so,\\
The equation of the line is given by
$$\myvec{-\frac{2}{9} & 1}\vec{x}= \frac{85}{9}$$
